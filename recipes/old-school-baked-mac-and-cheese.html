Old-School Baked Mac & Cheese

Description

Old-school baked mac & cheese is the ultimate comfort food, combining a creamy, cheesy sauce with perfectly cooked elbow macaroni, all baked to golden perfection. This classic recipe delivers nostalgia with every bite, featuring a rich, velvety cheese sauce made from scratch, topped with a buttery breadcrumb crust. Drawing inspiration from the beloved Costco mac & cheese, this version leans into a balance of sharp and mild cheeses for that creamy, stretchy consistency that makes it irresistible. The final bake ensures a crispy, bubbling top that contrasts beautifully with the soft, cheesy pasta underneath.

Whether you're making it for a family gathering or simply craving a cozy meal, this baked mac & cheese offers a hearty, satisfying dish that's simple yet packed with flavor. You'll love the rich combination of cheddar and mozzarella, along with the subtle nuttiness of Parmesan. A touch of cream cheese adds that extra Costco-style indulgence, making it feel extra special without any complicated steps.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb elbow macaroni
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted (for topping)

Steps

  1. Cook the Pasta: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Boil the elbow macaroni in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make the Roux: In a large saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until golden.
  3. Create the Cheese Sauce: Gradually whisk in the milk and heavy cream, stirring until thickened. Add the cheddar, mozzarella, Parmesan, and cream cheese. Stir until smooth.
  4. Combine and Assemble:Mix the cooked macaroni into the cheese sauce, ensuring it's evenly coated. Pour the mixture into a greased baking dish.
  5. Add Topping and Bake: Combine panko breadcrumbs with melted butter, then sprinkle over the macaroni. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown and bubbly. Enjoy!
